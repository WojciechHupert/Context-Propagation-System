BOUNDED CONTINUITY ARCHITECTURE

How Does It Work?

CPS works by turning moments into memory and memory into social consequence.

A conversation, event, or observation enters the system. CPS extracts what matters, stores it as structured memory, classifies who should know it, and decides whether it can move through the social network. When information spreads, the recipient does not receive a perfect copy. They receive a situated version: with source, uncertainty, emotional weight, and possible distortion. CPS Studio then makes the process visible, so the creator can inspect how knowledge moved, changed, or failed to spread.

The Four Layers

The interaction layer is where conversations, events, and observations enter. This can happen through a world like Lelit Distrikt, through Studio, or through another future interface.

The continuity layer is where CPS stores, classifies, routes, and mutates information. This is where memory becomes more than a transcript. It gains source, ownership, confidence, privacy, emotional weight, and history.

The observability layer is CPS Studio. This is where memory, lineage, propagation, and system behavior can be inspected. Without this layer, artificial social memory becomes a black box. With it, creators can see how information moved and why a later reaction happened.

The experience layer is where a world such as Lelit Distrikt makes those invisible processes feel real. A player does not experience a database. They experience a character who heard something, misunderstood something, protected a secret, or remembered a previous moment.

A Simple Flow

Someone says something. CPS extracts what matters. The system decides whether it is private, shareable, or public. If the information can travel, another character or entity receives its own version. Over time, that version can be recalled, distorted, emotionally reframed, or passed forward again. CPS Studio keeps the path visible.

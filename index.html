Context Propagation System

CPS supports environments where conversation has an afterlife. Information can be remembered, retold, withheld, distorted, and traced across a group instead of disappearing after one exchange.

CPS stands for Context Propagation System. It is infrastructure for worlds where information enters shared memory, moves between agents, and can later affect what happens next.

Not primarily. It can live inside a game-like world, but the system itself is about continuity: how information persists, spreads, and shapes later interactions.

CPS is not just about one character remembering the player. It is about what happens after that memory enters a wider social environment and starts to propagate.

AI systems are moving toward persistent memory and multi-agent workflows. Once information survives across time, it needs structure, boundaries, and visibility.

Social memory means information is part of the environment, not just a private note. It can have an origin, path, privacy boundary, mutation history, and later consequence.

Propagation is the movement of information between agents. CPS tracks how it travels, whether it should stay private, and whether later versions are duplicates or meaningful changes.

Persistent AI systems become opaque quickly. CPS exposes lineage, propagation paths, privacy rules, and runtime state so a human can inspect why something happened.

CPS Studio is the inspection surface. It shows what memories exist, where they came from, how they spread, and whether the system behaved as intended.

Lelit Distrikt is the current authored testbed for CPS. It gives the system a visible world where continuity, rumor, privacy, and consequence can be experienced.

The Social Life of Information

Information rarely moves as a perfect fact. It passes through trust, emotion, hierarchy, and retelling, then returns as rumor, warning, myth, or conflict.

CPS explores that process inside artificial worlds. It treats memory as shared infrastructure with source, path, privacy, confidence, and visible lineage. The central question is what the world remembers after something is said.

What Lelit Distrikt is about

Lelit Distrikt is a sealed world, a social experiment preserved beneath a dome. The outside world collapsed, but they were never told.

The project is an exploration of people, observation, and the weight of memory. It is a precise, intentional construction that creates a specific emotional experience. You aren't here to solve a puzzle or win a war; you are here to witness the texture of human life that was almost lost.

The town looks real, the characters feel real, and the sky above is a projection of a normality that no longer exists.

The first world built to make CPS visible.

Lelit Distrikt

Lelit Distrikt is not the whole product. It is the first demonstration world for CPS.

It is a theatre set, not a full life simulator. It exists to make invisible information dynamics visible through place, atmosphere, recurring characters, secrets, and consequence.

A Social Theatre for Memory

Lelit Distrikt gives CPS a stage. It is a bounded, authored district where memory and information flow can be experienced rather than merely described. The world does not try to simulate every aspect of life. It focuses on the social afterlife of conversation: what is remembered, what is shared, what is distorted, what remains hidden, and what returns later.

This is where CPS becomes emotionally legible. The system may track memory, lineage, and propagation, but Lelit turns those structures into moments: a character who heard something indirectly, a rumor that changed tone, a secret that stayed private, a conversation that matters because it did not end when the dialogue box closed.

Current Demonstration Goal

The current goal is deliberately focused. Lelit Distrikt is being used to prove one small but powerful loop: information enters the world, becomes structured memory, moves through a controlled social path, becomes visible in CPS Studio, and returns later through believable recall.

That is the foundation. Larger worlds, broader simulations, and deeper research scenarios can only become meaningful if this loop is stable, inspectable, and emotionally convincing.

Use Cases

CPS is for people who want to understand, design, or stage how information moves through a group.

It can be used in interactive worlds, narrative simulations, research prototypes, cultural installations, and experimental tools for studying artificial social behavior. The common thread is not genre. The common thread is continuity: what people or agents know, how they came to know it, how that knowledge changes, and what consequences it creates.

Interactive Worlds and Games

For interactive worlds and games, CPS can support characters who do not simply react in the moment, but carry traces of previous encounters, second-hand information, secrets, misunderstandings, and shifting trust. A player’s conversation can become part of the world’s social fabric instead of disappearing when the scene ends.

This creates a different kind of narrative possibility. A character may know something directly, hear it from someone else, doubt it, distort it, protect it, or bring it back later. The world begins to feel less like a sequence of isolated scenes and more like a place with social continuity.

Research and Social Simulation

For research and social simulation, CPS points toward controlled environments for exploring rumor propagation, institutional memory, belief formation, trust networks, emotional contagion, narrative pressure, and social fragmentation.

This is a long-term direction, not a claim of current scientific validation. The value is in creating bounded systems where information can be introduced, traced, compared, and studied. A research-oriented CPS would not need to simulate everything. It would need clear variables, repeatable conditions, constrained interventions, traceable memory, and visible propagation paths.

Museums, Galleries, and Cultural Institutions

For museums, galleries, and cultural institutions, CPS can support installations where visitors introduce ideas, events, or disruptions into an artificial community and later observe how those inputs echo through the system.

The work becomes less like a scripted interactive and more like a living social instrument. Visitors are not only choosing from prepared branches. They are placing information into a world and watching how that world absorbs, transforms, or resists it.

Writers and Worldbuilders

For writers and worldbuilders, CPS can act as a living continuity layer: a way to explore how a small community might respond to a secret, a crisis, a rumor, an authority, a threat, or a new shared belief.

Instead of manually scripting every reaction, a creator can design the conditions of social memory: who knows what, how reliable the information is, how emotionally charged it feels, and whether it can travel. CPS does not replace authorship. It gives authorship a social substrate.

Long-Term Research Platform

The long-term ambition is to move from a creative prototype toward a research-oriented platform for artificial social memory.

A research-grade CPS would not mean infinite realism, unrestricted autonomy, or a complete simulation of society. It would mean bounded artificial communities where specific social dynamics can be introduced, measured, repeated, and inspected. The focus is not on simulating everything. The focus is on making information flow observable.
